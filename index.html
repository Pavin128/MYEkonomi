Why Is Malaysia's Inflation Low?

Malaysia historically keeps CPI low through widespread fuel, electricity, and food subsidies ‚Äî costing ~RM 80B/year. The ongoing RON95 subsidy rationalisation introduced in 2024 will gradually shift to targeted subsidies, which may push inflation modestly higher. Bank Negara's OPR is held at 3.00% to balance growth and price stability.

üìå Leading Index

Composed of forward-looking components like stock prices, housing approvals, and export orders. A rising leading index signals economic expansion in the coming months.

üìå Coincident Index

Reflects the current state of the economy ‚Äî real output, employment, and income. Used to identify the current phase of the business cycle.

üìå Diffusion Index

A value of 100 means ALL components are rising. A value of 0 means ALL are falling. Values near 50 indicate balanced conditions across the economy.

What is MyEkonomi?

MyEkonomi is a free, public-facing economic dashboard for Malaysia. It fetches data directly from OpenDOSM ‚Äî the official open data platform of the Department of Statistics Malaysia ‚Äî using their public REST API. All data is licensed under CC BY 4.0, meaning it is freely shareable and reusable with attribution.

Data Update Frequency

Different datasets update at different frequencies:

‚Ä¢ Fuel prices ‚Äî Weekly (every Wednesday)
‚Ä¢ Labour force ‚Äî Monthly
‚Ä¢ CPI / Inflation ‚Äî Monthly
‚Ä¢ Economic Indicators ‚Äî Monthly
‚Ä¢ GDP data ‚Äî Annual / Quarterly
